The Crisis Context
Why UAE hotels are desperate for Indian volume right now
Occupancy crisis: Dubai hotels are running at 58–65% occupancy, down from 80%+ in 2022–23. Revenue per room has dropped 25–30% year-on-year. GMs have budget to discount heavily.
European/Russian gap: Traditional UAE source markets (Russia, UK, Germany) have dropped sharply. Hotels are actively reorienting to Indian sub-continent which is their fastest-growing segment.
Indian MICE boom: Corporate India is choosing Dubai over Singapore and Bangkok for incentive trips due to direct flights, Expo legacy venues, and no visa friction. First-mover operators capture preferred allotment.
📋
Must-Have Contract Terms
Non-negotiables for every UAE hotel contract
  • Net rates — minimum 25% margin FIT, 30% MICE/groups
  • No-deposit allotment — 5 rooms min for peak dates (Nov–Mar)
  • Free cancellation — 7 days FIT, 14 days groups
  • Indian meal plan — buffet with Indian section or à la carte
  • Halal food — already standard; confirm Indian vegetarian options
  • Early check-in / late check-out complimentary for ORN bookings
  • Hindi-speaking front desk or dedicated ORN contact
  • Group rates — 1 FOC per 15 paying pax minimum
  • MICE rates — include AV, coffee breaks, group transfer
  • Overriding commission — 3% quarterly above 100 room-nights
